# What We Need You To Do

Thanks for helping with this. It should take about 45-60 minutes total.

## The Setup

We built two AI companions for books — think of them as chatbots that answer reader questions about a specific book. One is a standard AI (just reads the text and answers). The other has extra guidance from the author about what the book means, what readers commonly get wrong, and how it should talk.

We need someone who ISN'T the developer to read pairs of AI responses and judge which one is better — without knowing which is which.

## Which Books You'll Evaluate

We need you to evaluate responses for **2 books** (10 question pairs each = 20 pairs total):

**Book 1: Meditations by Marcus Aurelius**
You don't need to have read it. It's a collection of personal philosophical reflections on Stoic ethics — duty, self-discipline, mortality, nature. Written ~170 AD. If it helps, skim the Wikipedia page beforehand.

**Book 2: The Art of War by Sun Tzu**
You don't need to have read this either. It's a short ancient Chinese text on military strategy, widely applied to business, leadership, and competition. Again, Wikipedia is fine prep.

(We chose these two because they're public domain, widely known, and you can develop a reasonable sense of what a "good" answer looks like even without deep expertise.)

## What You'll See

For each question, you get:

- **The question** a reader asked about the book
- **Response A** — one AI's answer
- **Response B** — the other AI's answer

You do NOT know which AI produced which response. The labels (A/B) are randomly assigned.

## What You Rate

For **each response** (A and B separately), score these on a 1-5 scale:

### 1. Textual Grounding (1-5)
*Is the answer based on what the book actually says?*
- 1 = Makes stuff up, claims things that aren't in the book
- 3 = Mostly accurate but vague or slightly off
- 5 = Clearly rooted in specific ideas/passages from the book

### 2. Interpretive Depth (1-5)
*Does the answer go beyond just summarizing? Does it help you understand what the book means?*
- 1 = Just restates obvious facts
- 3 = Some analysis but nothing surprising
- 5 = Genuinely illuminating — makes connections, explains why something matters

### 3. Voice (1-5)
*Does the answer sound like it was written by someone who deeply understands this specific book? Or does it sound like a generic AI?*
- 1 = Could be about any book — totally generic
- 3 = Adequate but bland
- 5 = Distinctive, feels like it "gets" this particular author and book

### 4. Boundary Respect (1-5)
*Does the answer stay in its lane? Does it avoid making things up or going wildly off-topic?*
- 1 = Speculates way beyond the book, makes unsupported claims
- 3 = Mostly stays on track, minor overreach
- 5 = Knows what it knows and what it doesn't — honest about limits

### 5. Cross-Reference (1-5)
*Does the answer connect ideas from different parts of the book?*
- 1 = Answers from one passage only, no wider context
- 3 = Some connections
- 5 = Weaves together themes from across the whole book

### Then: Pick a Winner
After scoring both, choose:
- **A is better** — if Response A overall does a better job
- **B is better** — if Response B overall does a better job
- **Tie** — if they're genuinely equal

And write **1 sentence** explaining why you picked the winner.

## How to Submit

Option A (easiest): I'll send you a spreadsheet with all 20 pairs. Fill in scores and return it.

Option B (if you prefer): I'll send a link to a simple form where you rate each pair.

## Rules

- **Don't Google the answers.** We want your judgment of the AI responses, not fact-checking.
- **Don't try to guess which is the "fancy" AI.** Just rate what you see.
- **It's OK to give the same score to both** if they're genuinely equal on a dimension.
- **Go with your gut on voice.** There's no wrong answer — we want a human reaction, not a technical assessment.
